How to Maintain Your Backpack

Make your gear last a lifetime.

Good gear deserves good care.
Table of Contents
  • 1. Clean it Regularly
  • 2. Zip Care
  • 3. Store it Right
  • Frequently Asked Questions

Your backpack is your most important travel companion. Taking care of it ensures it will take care of you on the trail. Here are some simple maintenance tips.

1. Clean it Regularly

After a long trip, empty every pocket and shake out the dirt. For a deeper clean, wash it by hand in a bathtub with warm water and mild soap. Never use a washing machine as it can strip the waterproof coating.

Read Also: The Ultimate Guide to Packing Light

2. Zip Care

Zippers are often the first thing to fail. Keep them clean from grit and sand. If a zipper gets stuck, try lubricating it with a little graphite (like a pencil) or a specialized zipper lubricant.

"Take care of your gear, and your gear will take care of you." – Old Hiker Adage
Store your pack clean and dry ready for the next adventure.

3. Store it Right

Store your backpack in a cool, dry place out of specific sunlight. UV rays can degrade the fabric over time. Make sure it's completely dry before storing to prevent mold and mildew.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use detergent?

Standard laundry detergent is too harsh. Use a product like Nikwax Tech Wash or mild baby soap.

How do I re-waterproof my bag?

You can use a spray-on chemical water repellant treatment (DWR) after washing and drying your bag.
